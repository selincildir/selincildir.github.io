Projects

Genetic Architecture of Lipoedema: Rare-Variant Mapping across Lymphatic Development, Connective-Tissue Biology, and Adipose Distribution

Status: Ongoing

Supervisors: A/Prof. Chung Hoow Kok, Prof. Hamish Scott, Prof. Natasha Harvey, and Dr Kelly Betterman

Summary:This project investigates the genetic basis of lipoedema, a chronic and complex adipose‑tissue disorder characterised by disproportionate, painful fat accumulation and frequently associated with lymphatic and connective‑tissue abnormalities. Despite its prevalence and clinical burden, the molecular mechanisms underlying lipoedema remain poorly defined. The overarching aim of this research is to advance foundational understanding of the disease's genetic architecture and identify biological pathways that may contribute to its development and progression. This work is undertaken as part of my Master of Research at Adelaide University within the College of Health and Medical Sciences.

Cloud-Integrated Building Management System for Environmental Monitoring in Medical Laboratories Using Node-RED

Status: Completed

Supervisor: Associate Professor Alireza Jolfaei, Cybersecurity and Networking in the College of Science and Engineering, Flinders University, Adelaide, South Australia, Australia

Summary:As part of a Cloud and Distributed Computing course at Flinders University, I developed a cloud-integrated Building Management System (BMS) tailored for medical laboratory environments using Node-RED. This system simulates temperature, humidity and air-quality readings, validates and classifies them against safety thresholds, and then seamlessly logs and visualises the results while triggering alerts when conditions fall outside acceptable ranges. Simulated sensor data is generated (20–35 °C for temperature, 30–70 % for humidity, 0–150 AQI) and processed via JavaScript function nodes that flag extremes and categorise air quality as 'Good', 'Moderate' or 'Poor'; readings breaching preset thresholds (e.g. >30 °C, <35 % or >65 % humidity, AQI >100) automatically generate email notifications through Gmail SMTP. Concurrently, each data point is written in tabular form to Google Sheets and stored as structured JSON documents in Google Firestore, while a live Node-RED Dashboard radar chart provides real-time visual feedback.

Investigating Immune Function in IKKα: T and B Cell Profiling in Health and Disease via Genetic Demultiplexing of scRNA-Seq

Status: Completed

Supervisor: Dr. Nick Warnock, Centre for Cancer Biology, Adelaide, South Australia, Australia

Summary:This study employed single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) and genetic demultiplexing to investigate immune function in individuals with the G167R homozygous germline mutation in the IKKα (CHUK) gene (chr10:100219335). Peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) were used for both bulk RNA-seq and scRNA-seq experiments, with bulk RNA-seq serving as a genetic reference to assign cells to their respective donors (HC1, HC2, HC3, Patient1) in the scRNA-seq dataset. The pipeline involved STAR for bulk RNA-seq read alignment, VarDict for SNP calling from bulk RNA-seq, and cellSNP-lite for SNP calling from scRNA-seq. Vireo was then used to demultiplex pooled scRNA-seq samples based on the detected SNPs. The Cell Ranger Multi Pipeline (v9.0.0) was implemented to generate the feature-barcode matrix, which was subsequently analyzed using Seurat in R. The study applied various R packages, including dplyr for data manipulation, ggplot2 and patchwork for visualization, scDblFinder for doublet detection, Azimuth for cell type annotation, and tibble for structured data handling. The primary objective was to analyze T and B cell heterogeneity and perform cell-type-specific differential gene expression analysis, focusing on the impact of the homozygous CHUK mutation on NF-κB signaling, immune cell composition, and transcriptional differences across healthy and disease conditions.

Smart Manufacturing Dashboard: An Intuitive Web App for Monitoring and Managing Factory Operations

Status: Completed

Supervisor: Dr. Brett Wilkinson, Senior Lecturer in Computer Science and Information Technology, Flinders University, Adelaide, South Australia, Australia

Summary:Developed a web-based dashboard for real-time monitoring and management of factory operations, designed for Administrators, Factory Managers, Production Operators, and Auditors. The platform provides CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) functionalities for managing machines, jobs, and maintenance requests, featuring real-time alerts and performance analytics to support data-driven decision-making. This project was undertaken as part of the "Human Factors for Interactive and Web-Based Systems" topic during my second year in the Bachelor of Science degree at Flinders University. Collaborating with a team of four peers, we built and deployed the web application within three months, utilizing: Technologies HTML, PHP, JavaScript, SQL; Deployment: XAMPP server; Collaboration Platforms: GitHub, Miro. The project successfully demonstrated team-based software development, UI/UX considerations, and real-time data integration in a manufacturing context.
